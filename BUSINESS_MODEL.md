# DeerPark — Business Model & Pricing

Internal. Not for the public site yet.

## Operating shape

Solo operator. Four revenue lines, sequenced so consulting funds the product.

```
Discovery sprint  →  Build engagement  →  Operator retainer  →  Vertical product
   (filter)            (cash engine)         (recurring)           (compounding asset)
```

## Pricing

| Line | Price | Shape | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Discovery sprint** | £2,500 | 1 week, fixed-fee | Paid scoping. Credit against Build within 60 days. Filters tire-kickers and gets paid to write the SOW. |
| **Build engagement** | £18,000 | 6 weeks, fixed-fee | One workflow, end-to-end. 50% upfront, 50% on handoff. Triples the prior £2–8K floor on purpose — reprices out of the Zapier-cowboy bracket. |
| **Operator retainer** | £2,000 / month | 1 day/wk equivalent | Sold on every Build close. Eval monitoring, tuning, minor changes. Annual prepay = 11 months for 10. Target 60% attach. |
| **Vertical product** | £400 / month | SaaS, annual contract | One of the three products in development. Pick the one whose ICP overlaps the Build pipeline. |

## Capacity caps (solo reality)

- **Builds**: 6 per year (£108K)
- **Retainers**: 5 concurrent (£120K ARR ceiling)
- **Discoveries**: 12 per year (£30K, plus pipeline)
- **Product**: ramp to ~30 paying customers by Y2 (~£144K ARR)

When full: "next slot opens [month]" — scarcity, not discount.

## Realistic revenue path

- **Y1**: ~£210K total (£180K consulting + £30K product), exit ARR ~£60K
- **Y2**: ~£350K total, ~50% recurring, exit ARR ~£200K
- **Y3 (if product compounds)**: £500–700K, mostly recurring

## Positioning

Not "AI consultant." **AI-enabled cloud engineering for [one UK vertical, TBD].**

The moat is depth + niche + integration, not tool knowledge. Tool knowledge has a 6-month shelf life; vertical reputation and shipped systems do not.

## Rules to enforce against self

1. **No off-menu work.** If it doesn't fit Discovery / Build / Retainer, it's a no.
2. **No hourly.** Ever. Even for "small favours" or warm intros.
3. **Discovery is mandatory before any Build quote.** No exceptions.
4. **Don't discount, sequence.** £18K too much? Sell them a £2.5K Discovery first. Buyers earn the right to the Build.
5. **Cap is public.** "6 Build slots per year, 2 remaining for Q3" sells better than any case study.
6. **One vertical.** Pick it within 30 days. Without it the £18K Build won't hold against cheaper generalists.

## Open decisions

1. **The vertical.** Which past/current clients cluster tightest? That's the beachhead. Candidates worth scoring: UK accountancies, FCA-regulated fintech, NHS-adjacent providers, UK manufacturing with legacy ERP.
2. **First product to push.** Of the three in development, the one whose buyer is identical to the Build buyer — distribution is the lever, only spend it once.
3. **Y1 revenue target.** Decide the number so it's clear when to say no vs. when to hustle.
